Community Engagement Summary

This summary highlights the community engagement activities conducted to raise awareness and promote the use of the new DTL collections.

Engagement Activities

  • Workshops: Conducted workshops for community members and scholars to introduce the new collections and demonstrate how to access and utilize them.
  • Seminars: Organized seminars focusing on the importance of digital collections in religious studies and the role of the DTL in providing access to these resources.
  • Collaborations: Partnered with local religious studies organizations and academic institutions to promote the collections and gather feedback for improvement.

Key Learnings

  • Community Needs: Gained a deeper understanding of the needs and preferences of the community regarding digital collections.
  • Effective Communication: Improved skills in effectively communicating the value and use of digital collections to diverse audiences.

Challenges

  • Engagement: Ensuring active participation and engagement from community members in the workshops and seminars.
  • Feedback Collection: Gathering comprehensive and actionable feedback from community members to improve the collections.

Next Steps

  • Follow-Up Workshops: Plan and conduct follow-up workshops to address any issues and provide additional training.
  • Feedback Implementation: Analyze the feedback collected and implement changes to improve the collections and their accessibility.

Examples

  • Successful Workshop: Conducted a successful workshop with over 30 participants, receiving positive feedback on the usefulness of the new collections and suggestions for additional resources.
