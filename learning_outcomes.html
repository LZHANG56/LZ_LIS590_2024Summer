Learning Outcomes and Program-Level Outcome Mapping

Outcome 1

Learning Outcome: Develop and manage a specialized collection focusing on Chinese and Korean religious studies for the DTL, ensuring diverse perspectives are represented.

Program-Level Outcomes: 1 (Information Management), 2 (Knowledge Organization), 6 (Technological Proficiency)

iSchool Values Reflection: This project promotes equity and diversity by broadening the library's collection to include underrepresented groups and viewpoints, thereby supporting the iSchool's commitment to inclusivity and accessibility.

Project/Task Description:

  • Identify and acquire key texts, multimedia, and scholarly articles related to Chinese and Korean religious studies that are not currently available on the DTL website.
  • Collaborate with subject matter experts and community members to ensure cultural accuracy and comprehensiveness.
  • Use OCLC knowledge base tools to catalog and organize the collection, ensuring metadata standards are maintained.

Evidence/Criteria for Evaluation:

  • Creation of a fully cataloged collection in the DTL database with detailed metadata entries for each item.
  • Positive feedback from DTL librarians and community representatives on the collection’s relevance and cultural accuracy.

Outcome 2

Learning Outcome: Master the OCLC knowledge base functionalities to enhance the discoverability and accessibility of new and existing collections.

Program-Level Outcomes: 3 (Data Practices), 9 (Problem Solving)

iSchool Values Reflection: Enhances accessibility and supports equity by improving user experience and ensuring that diverse audiences can easily access resources.

Project/Task Description:

  • Receive training on advanced features of the OCLC knowledge base, focusing on digital collection management and troubleshooting.
  • Regularly update and refine the search functionalities to ensure easy access and navigability.

Evidence/Criteria for Evaluation:

  • Documented improvements in search and retrieval functions, verified through user feedback and analytics.
  • Logs of troubleshooting activities detailing the problems identified, actions taken, and results achieved.

Outcome 3

Learning Outcome: Cultivate effective intercultural communication and collaboration skills by engaging with communities related to the Chinese and Korean religious studies collections.

Program-Level Outcomes: 4 (User Engagement), 7 (Community Connection)

iSchool Values Reflection: Promotes sovereignty by valuing the voices of the community in the collection development process, ensuring their perspectives are respected and accurately represented.

Project/Task Description:

  • Organize and facilitate focus groups and feedback sessions with community members to understand their needs and expectations from the collection.
  • Develop and implement outreach programs to promote the collection, including workshops, seminars, and online content tailored to the interests of Chinese and Korean communities.

Evidence/Criteria for Evaluation:

  • Detailed reports and reflections on each community engagement activity, including participant feedback and personal learnings.
  • Examples of promotional materials and online content created for the collection, demonstrating effective communication and outreach.

Outcome 4

Learning Outcome: Implement an effective metadata schema tailored for the Chinese and Korean religious studies collections to facilitate efficient cataloging and retrieval.

Program-Level Outcomes: 2 (Knowledge Organization), 6 (Technological Proficiency)

iSchool Values Reflection: This outcome supports the iSchool's values of accessibility and equity by creating a metadata framework that respects cultural specifics and enhances the usability of the collection.

Project/Task Description:

  • Develop a customized metadata schema that addresses the specific needs of Chinese and Korean religious texts, incorporating input from linguistic and cultural experts.
  • Develop the schema with a subset of the collection, cataloging key texts to test its effectiveness.

Evidence/Criteria for Evaluation:

  • A detailed report on the metadata schema developed, including justifications for the choices made based on cultural and linguistic considerations.
  • Feedback from a sample review by DTL librarians and subject matter experts focusing on the schema’s functionality and accuracy.

Outcome 5

Learning Outcome: Acquire and apply digital preservation techniques to ensure the longevity and integrity of the Chinese and Korean religious studies digital collections.

Program-Level Outcomes: 1 (Information Management), 9 (Problem Solving)

iSchool Values Reflection: Enhances the sovereignty of the community by ensuring their cultural heritage is preserved accurately and accessibly, aligning with the iSchool's commitment to sustainability and long-term access.

Project/Task Description:

  • Train in digital preservation methods suitable for the specific types of media and content found in the collections, such as manuscripts, digital images, and recordings.
  • Implement these techniques on a selected portion of the digital assets to assess their effectiveness in preserving the content long-term.

Evidence/Criteria for Evaluation:

  • A preservation log detailing the methods applied, challenges encountered, and solutions implemented.
  • Evaluation report assessing the impact of the preservation techniques on the sample materials’ integrity and accessibility.
